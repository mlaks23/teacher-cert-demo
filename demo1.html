Section 1: Painting

  • In 1933, Diego Rivera, a Mexican muralist, was commissioned by Nelson Rockefeller to paint a mural in 30 Rockefeller Plaza
  • There were three panals showing scientific progress.

Section 2: Controversy

  • In the center panel, Rivera painted a large picture of Valdimir Lenin.
  • This was considered to be communist propaganda.
  • Rockefeller asked Rivera to remove the painting of Lenin, but he would not.

Section 3: Destruction

  • Instead of painting over the portrait of Lenin, the mural was destroyed.
  • The night before it was destroyed, Lucienne Bloch, photographer and friend of Rivera, took pictures of the mural.
  • A smaller version of the mural was painted in the Palazzo de Bellas Artes in Mexico City.
  • A few years later, a different artist was commissioned to make a new mural, which is still there today.

Section 4: Connection with My Grandmother, Isabel Blumenfeld

In 1933 my grandmother was about 17 years old and living in the Bronx. A friend of hers read that Diego Rivera was painting a mural in Rockefeller Center. My grandmother and her friend went to see the mural being painted. Rivera saw her and invited her to come up to the scaffold. He asked her name and then drew her into the painting. She told me Frida Kahlo, Rivera's wife and a Japanaese artist, possibly Isamu Nogochi, were there. Around 2005, I took my grandmother to Rockefeller Center. I spoke with a guard and asked him about the mural. She described to us where the scaffold was and where the paintings were. The guard said she was correct in her reccollections
